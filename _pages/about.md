About me
======

Hi, I’m Rafik. I’m a *maître de conférences* (~associate professor) at Université Polytechnique Hauts-de-France and a member of the [LAMIH lab (UMR CNRS 8201)](https://www.uphf.fr/lamih/presentation/qui-sommes-nous). Before that, I was a postdoc in the [DIVA group](https://diva.telecom-paristech.fr/) at [Télécom Paris](https://www.telecom-paris.fr/). I received my Ph.D. in 2020 at [Inria Bordeaux](https://www.inria.fr/fr/centre-inria-universite-bordeaux) in the [PHOENIX team](https://radar.inria.fr/rapportsactivite/RA2017/phoenix/uid4.html).

My research is in Human–Computer Interaction. I develop and evaluate human-centered design approaches for intelligent systems, informed by human factors. My work focuses on context-aware and autonomous intelligent systems that support human activity and decision making. My research adopts an interdisciplinary approach, at the intersection of computer science, psychology, and human factors. <!-- in Computer Science... My research takes place within an interdisciplinary approach, as I collaborate with researchers in the Humanities and Social Sciences with a focus on making intelligent systems easier to design and use in ways that meet people’s needs. I apply this in domains like assistive technologies and explainable AI.to help non-expert users understand complex systems. My research takes place within an interdisciplinary approach, while I collaborate with researchers in the Humanities and Social Sciences, in particular in psychology and human factors.
